Algebra Expressions

Algebraic Expression:

An algebraic expression is a mathematical statement that combines variables, constants, and mathematical operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division).
Example: 3x - 2y, -⅖ x2 - 3yz + z, 2mn, 6x, 5a - 2bc, etc.

Coefficient part and Literal part:

In the expression 3x - 2y, 3 is the coefficient part and x is the literal part(it is always non-numerical).
Example: In 4abc,
coefficient of abc is 4,
the coefficient of bc is 4a,
the coefficient of c is 4ab.

Constants and Variables: The numbers used in the expressions (3x - 2y, -⅖ x2 - 3yz + z, 2mn, 6x) 3, -2, -⅖ , -3 , 1, 2 and 6 are called constants and x, y, z, m, n, a, b, c are called variables.

Terms:

Parts of an expression connected by signs + and - are called terms of the expression.
Example:

  • 3x and 2y are terms of the expression 3x - 2y,
  • -⅖ x2, 3yz and z are the terms of the expression -⅖ x2 - 3yz + z.
  • 2mn is only one term.

Monomial, Binomial, Trinomial:



Monomial: An expression having only one term is called a monomial.
Example: 2x, 7a, 25y2, -3/7yz

Binomial: An expression having two terms is called a binomial.
Example: 2x+3y, 7a-bc, x + 25y2, -3/7yz +xz

Trinomial: An expression having three terms is called a trinomial.
Example: -3yz + xz + 5xy, -7x + 3y - 4z, 7a - bc - 4c, 2x - 2y + 25y2

An expression having more than 3 terms is called a multi-nominal.

Monomial, binomial, trinomial and multinomial are called polynomials.

  • 5 is also a polynomial having one term.

NOTE: For an algebraic expression to be a polynomial, the powers of the variables should be whole numbers
Example: 2x2 + 7z is a polynomial
2/y + 5x is not a polynomial.
5√x + 7xy is not a polynomial.

Like and Unlike terms:

Like terms are terms that have the same variables raised to the same powers.

Examples:

  • 3x and 5x (both have the variable 'x' to the power of 1)
  • -2y² and 7y² (both have the variable 'y' to the power of 2)
  • 4a²b and -10ba² (both have the variables 'a' and 'b' to the power of 2 and 1 respectively)

Unlike terms are terms that have different variables or the same variables raised to different powers.
Examples:
3x and 5y (different variables)
2x² and 7x (same variable, but different powers)
4ab and -10a (different variables)

  • If an algebraic expression contains only one variable, say x, it is called an algebraic expression in one variable.
    Example: 3x, 7x², 5x + 7x3
  • If an algebraic expression contains two variables, say x and y, it is called an algebraic expression in two variables.
    Example: xy3, 7x²y, 5xy - 3x3
  • If an algebraic expression contains three variables, say x, y and z, it is called an algebraic expression in three variables.
    Example: xy3z, -2x²yz, 5xy - 3/7x3z

Addition of Algebraic Expressions:

To add algebraic expressions, we follow these steps:

  1. Identify Like Terms:
    • Like terms have the same variables raised to the same powers.
    • For example, 3x and 5x are like terms, while 3x and 5y are not.
  2. Combine Like Terms:
    • Add or subtract the coefficients of the like terms. The variables and their exponents remain the same.
    • Example: 3x + 5x = 8x

Example: Add (3x² + 2x - 5) and (5x² - 4x + 1)

  1. Identify Like Terms:
    • 3x² and 5x² (both have x²)
    • 2x and -4x (both have x)
    • -5 and 1 (both are constants)
  2. (3x² + 5x²) + (2x - 4x) + (-5 + 1) = 8x² - 2x - 4
    Therefore, (3x² + 2x - 5) + (5x² - 4x + 1) = 8x² - 2x - 4

Column Method: Add (3x² - 5 + 2x) and (5x² - 4x + 1)
We arrange the expressions in lines so that the like terms may be in the same vertical column, then add each column beginning with the left. 


            
            3x² + 2x - 5
            5x² - 4x + 1
           ---------------
            8x² - 2x - 4
           ---------------

Subtraction of Algebraic Expressions:

To subtract algebraic expressions, you follow these steps:

  • Change the signs of each term in the expression being subtracted.
  • Combine like terms and add it to the expression from which subtraction was to be made.

Example: Subtract 8x from 10x.

  • If you're subtracting 8x, it becomes -8x.
  • Now we add it to the expression: 10x + (-8x) = 10x - 8x = 2x

Example: Subtract (5x² - 4x + 1) from (3x² + 2x - 5)

  1. Change the signs of the terms in the expression being subtracted:
    • (5x² - 4x + 1) becomes (-5x² + 4x - 1)
  2. Combine Like Terms:
    • (3x² + 2x - 5) + (-5x² + 4x - 1)
    • = (3x² - 5x²) + (2x + 4x) + (-5 - 1)
    • = -2x² + 6x - 6
    Therefore, (3x² + 2x - 5) - (5x² - 4x + 1) = -2x² + 6x - 6

Column Method of Subtraction: Subtract (5x² - 4x + 1) from (3x² - 5 + 2x)

Step 1: Combine Like Terms
We combine the like terms vertically. 

            3x² + 2x - 5

Step 2: Change Signs of the Subtrahend
When subtracting expressions, we change the signs of all terms in the expression being subtracted. 

                      
                      3x² + 2x - 5 
                      5x² - 4x + 1
                    -     +    -
                       ----------------

                   ----------------

Therefore, (3x² + 2x - 5) - (5x² - 4x + 1) = -2x² + 6x - 6
